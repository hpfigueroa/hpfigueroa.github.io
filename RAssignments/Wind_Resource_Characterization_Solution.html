Wind Resource Characterization

MECE 4210 Energy Infrastructure Planning

To determine wind power productivity and wind resource characterization, wind speed measurements over long periods (months and/or years) are usually used and most of the time required. The wind power production is dependent not only on wind speed but also on wind turbine efficiency and wind farm geometry arrangement. We will learn later that curtailment is also important in the power production equation. To simplify this assignment we will analyze the power of only one wind turbine.

The following datasets are available:
* Three years (2012, 2013 and 2014) of wind speed measurements from Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory at 52m height at full resolution or 15 minute sampling time.
* Two power curves for a representative IEC 61400 Class 2 and Class 3 wind turbine with 90m diameter and 1.5MW power rating. These curves, among others, are available in the System Advisory Model (SAM) software from NREL.
